Observation 01

BecomingComparable.

Nothing about the three places changed.

Yet they became easier to compare.

The places remained distinct.

The comparison did not.

Places are not compared.
Their representations are.

Comparing everything at once would be impossible.

So the mind looks for what different options share.

Price with price. Rating with rating. Location with location.

Once those common points appear, the decision becomes easier.

Psychology calls this structural alignment.

When the researchers who first described it needed an everyday example, they reached for the most natural pair they could find: a hotel, and a motel.

The science of comparison was already about places to stay.

Luxury is often valuable for reasons that are difficult to measure. Quietness. Privacy. Care. A sense of place.

PriceRatingsAmenitiesPoliciesLocation

But choices are usually organized through what is easy to compare.

The question is no longer whether a place is different.

It is whether that difference survives comparison.

A remarkable place can become an ordinary choice if its most valuable qualities never become perceptible before arrival.

Method & sources

The three desires in this piece — quietness, privacy, restoration — are the most consistently documented motivations behind getaway travel. The three options are not real properties: they stand for millions. Their figures are representative, informed by aggregate short-term rental market data, so that no single place is being judged.

Nothing about you was measured. No choice was recorded. Nothing you did was right or wrong — that is the point.

Gentner, D. & Markman, A. B. — Structure Mapping in Analogy and Similarity (1997)The researchers' own worked example of alignment is the comparison of a hotel and a motel: similar enough that their differences become easy to list.

Gentner, D. & Markman, A. B. — No Difference Without Similarity (1994)Differences are easier to notice between things that share structure — the more comparable two options become, the more visible their differences along shared dimensions, and only those.
