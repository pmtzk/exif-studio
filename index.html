Why EXIF

A hospitality operator who produces the imagery, not a photographer who happens to like hotels.

EXIF came to hospitality through the floor. Years inside resorts across sales, guest experience and operations, where the daily question was why a guest chooses one property over another, books direct or does not, returns or does not.

Most people who understand how a property earns its rate cannot produce the imagery. Most people who can produce the imagery have never sat inside the operation. EXIF does both, so the work starts with how the property is perceived and how it books, and the images follow from there.

The difference

A photographer makes the rooms look correct. EXIF makes the property communicate its value. Only one of those moves bookings.

How we work

Every engagement begins by understanding the property before changing anything about it.

We study the property the way a guest experiences it, and the way a booking decision actually gets made. We note what is distinctive about being there, and where the current perception falls short of the reality.

The Atmosphere Audit puts that read on paper. A scored, specific diagnosis of how the property is perceived across site, OTAs and social, and where it is costing bookings and rate.

We decide what needs to change and in what order, prioritized by commercial impact rather than by what is easiest to shoot.

We create and deliver the imagery as an organized system, mapped to where each asset works hardest across every channel a guest passes through.

Behind EXIF

EXIF is led by Katia, who has spent years inside hospitality across Mexico and the Caribbean, in resort sales, guest experience, operations and photography. The studio exists because of a pattern she saw from the inside: strong properties, consistently undersold by how they are perceived online. EXIF was built to close that gap, and to treat perception as what it is for a property, a commercial asset rather than a gallery.
